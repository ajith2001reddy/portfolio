Translating Infrastructure Complexity

The Challenge

At TCS, the Data Platform Operations team managed complex infrastructure that generated thousands of daily technical alerts. Non-technical stakeholders and internal users struggled to interpret these alerts, leading to delayed resolutions and "communication silos" where engineering intent was lost in translation.

The Strategy

I acted as the bridge between system engineers and operational users. My strategy was to deconstruct raw system logs and infrastructure concepts into a standard "Health-to-Action" documentation format. I interviewed the engineering teams to capture the root causes behind common alerts and translated that into actionable, user-facing instructions.

The Solution & Documentation

I created a centralized internal documentation hub that simplified technical workflows. Instead of raw server logs, I developed "Status Summaries" that explained:
1. What is happening? (The technical state)
2. Why does it matter? (Business impact)
3. What do I do? (Actionable next steps)
This reduced the time spent "deciphering" alerts and enabled non-technical operators to initiate troubleshooting independently.

Impact

The new documentation framework improved cross-functional collaboration and operational efficiency. By simplifying technical instructions, we reduced the reliance on senior engineering time for routine status checks and empowered the wider operations team with clear, accessible knowledge guides.
